FAQ

My kefir smells funky (or off)

Yep! It's probably fine, though.

It smells worst right as you're straining and bottling it. It's warm, sour, yeasty, and full of carbon dioxide. Mine smells like a cross between warm beer and yoghurt at this stage. It'll smell less funky once it's cold, and some lemon peel in the second ferment really helps cut the funk as well.

Another possibility: sunlight (or any kind of light) can make milk products taste weird, think cheese or butter left on the bench for a few hours, so make sure your kefir is in a dark place or an opaque container.

Part of the difficulty is that we're not used to leaving milk out of the fridge so weird smells seem scary. I've never had any milk kefir go off as healthy grains will populates the milk very rapidly and prevent any other bacteria or moulds from getting started. Remember, fridges haven't been around very long! I've read that milk kefir lasts for weeks unrefrigerated, though I've never tried it myself.

Of course, there is always a chance that you legitimately have an off batch. This could happen because your grains are very weak or dead, or your milk was already off. You could try giving your grains a good wash in cold tap water and making another batch, or just throw the grains out and get some fresh ones. Milk kefir is very robust so this seems unlikely to me, but it's always a possibility with fermenting.

My kefir has split/separated

That's fine, it's a little over-fermented but not off. Shake the kefir up to combine it again and strain it as usual.

Shaking will increase the pressure (like shaking a bottle of lemonade) so open the container in the sink to avoid explosions and be extra careful if you're using a glass container that isn't made to withstand pressure.

It might taste a bit stronger or more acidic than normal but there's nothing wrong with it. Primary fermentation speeds up in warmer temperatures so you can try keeping it in a cooler part of the house or reducing the fermentation time if you'd rather avoid this.
